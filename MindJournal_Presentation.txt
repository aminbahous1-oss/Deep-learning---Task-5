SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Why do we need a smart journal?

  • 1 in 4 people struggle to recognize and track their own emotional patterns
  • Traditional journaling gives no feedback or actionable insights
  • Mental health check-ins are often skipped because they feel tedious
  • People lack tools that bridge self-reflection and data-driven awareness

  Goal: Make emotional self-awareness effortless and data-driven


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SLIDE 3 — WHAT IS MINDJOURNAL?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Our Solution — A web-based smart journal that:

  • Lets you speak or type how you feel — no friction
  • Analyzes your emotions from both your text and your face (webcam)
  • Tracks your mood over time with interactive visual charts
  • Requires zero mental health expertise to use
  • Works instantly — no sign-up, runs entirely in your browser

SLIDE 6 — FEATURE 1: JOURNAL ENTRY PAGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

URL: http://localhost:5000

  LEFT COLUMN — Journal Entry:
    • Large text area for typing or dictating the journal entry
    • [🎤 Start Recording] button — uses browser Speech API
    • [📸 Capture Mood]   button — takes webcam snapshot
    • [Analyze Entry]     button — sends text to IBM Watson NLU
    • Status message: "✓ Entry saved!" after analysis

  RIGHT COLUMN — Results:
    • Live webcam feed
    • Face Emotion result  e.g. "😊 Joy (82%)"
    • Emotion bars (colored by type):
